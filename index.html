The Problem

A critical gap between discharge and detection

Post-cesarean surgical site infections typically manifest 4–10 days after surgery — well after the standard 2–4 day inpatient stay. New mothers go home before complications appear.

Day 2–4

Patient discharged from hospital. Wound appears normal.

Day 4–10

Detection gap. SSI develops undetected. New mother is alone, sleep-deprived, focused on newborn care.

Day 10–14

Infection severe enough for self-referral or readmission. Diagnosis delayed 3–7 days — worsening outcomes.

Maternal health burden

SSIs exacerbate postpartum depression, impede maternal bonding, and extend recovery. Women deserve better monitoring during their most vulnerable weeks.

Healthcare system costs

SSI-complicated cases cost €19,008 vs €9,040 for uncomplicated cases. Readmissions average €7,962 per episode. Up to 24,000 cases per year in Germany alone.

No condition-specific tools

Existing digital wound monitoring platforms target chronic wounds or general postoperative populations. None are designed specifically for post-cesarean SSI surveillance with a postpartum-tailored user experience.

Our Solution

AI-powered monitoring built exclusively for post-cesarean care

MIRRA Recovery is the first digital health platform combining a condition-specific deep learning algorithm, multimodal symptom and image analysis, and a postpartum-tailored user experience — designed to detect SSIs 24–48 hours after clinical manifestation.

  • Post-cesarean-specific AI — trained exclusively on annotated wound photographs from cesarean cohorts, not generic wound datasets
  • Multimodal analysis — combines wound image AI with daily symptom check-ins for higher sensitivity and specificity
  • Postpartum-tailored UX — addresses body image sensitivity, sleep deprivation context, and newborn care demands
  • EU MDR Class IIa regulatory pathway — CE marking and DiGA listing targeted by Month 30–36

How It Works

From hospital discharge to confident recovery

A 14-day monitoring journey designed around a new mother's real-life constraints.

01

Onboarding at discharge

Clinical staff enrol patients before leaving hospital. The app is configured with the patient's wound profile and care pathway.

→
02

Daily wound photo + symptom check

Patients upload a guided photo and answer a brief symptom checklist each day. Designed to take under 2 minutes.

→
03

AI analysis & risk score

MIRRA's deep learning model analyses each submission and generates a risk score combining image findings with symptom data.

→
04

Clinician alert & action

Elevated risk scores trigger immediate notifications to the clinical team — enabling early intervention before symptoms become severe.
